Pad Kee Mao (Thai Drunken Noodles)

★★★★

Prep Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 15 minutes Difficulty: Medium Servings: Servings: 4 Source: Carlsbadcravings.com

Ingredients

CHICKEN

12 ounces chicken thigh

1 tbsp reduced sodium soy sauce

1 tsp toasted sesame oil

1 tsp cornstarch


NOODLES

8 ounces wide rice noodles


VEG

4 cloves garlic, minced

½ white onion, thinly sliced

1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced

½ cup grape tomatoes, halved

1 cup fresh basil


NOODLE SAUCE (for 8 ounces of noodles)


3 tbsp oyster sauce

3 tbsp reduced sodium soy sauce

2 tbsp dark brown sugar

1 tbsp rice vinegar

1 tsp molasses

1/2 tbsp fish sauce

2 tsp chili paste like Sambal Oelek (optional)

1/2 tsp ground coriander

1/2 tsp ground ginger

1/4 tsp pepper


OPTIONAL

- Add 4 chopped Thai peppers to make it a drunk noodle recipe or put more chili sauce in

- add beaten egg at end

Directions

MARINATE CHICKEN
- Whisk together 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon sesame oil and 1 teaspoon cornstarch in a medium bowl
- Add chopped chicken and toss to evenly coat; set aside.

PREPARE RICE NOODLES
- Place the wide rice noodles in a large bowl and pour boiling water over top until completely submerged and give them a good stir
- Let them soak until softened, pliable, and limp but not fully tender/still al dente, stirring occasionally so they don’t stick together, about 12-15 minutes (less for thinner noodles)
- Rinse noodles in cold water, while separating any stubborn noodles with your fingers
- Drain very well and toss with 1 teaspoon sesame oil

PREPARE SAUCE
- Whisk all of the Sauce Ingredients together in a medium bowl

STIRFRY CHICKEN
- Heat 2 tablespoons vegetable oil in a wok over high heat or large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat
- Once very hot, add chicken, onion, and chilies (if using) and cook for 2-3 minutes
- Add bell peppers, corn and garlic and stir fry 2-3 minutes

STIRFRY NOODLES
- Put chicken mixture aside
- IF USING A WOK, add 1 Tablespoon additional oil and heat over high heat
- IF USING A NONSTICK SKILLET, heat 1 teaspoon additional oil over medium-high heat (the oil helps prevent the noodles from sticking)
- Add the noodles and sauce to the pan and toss to combine
- Spread the noodles over the surface of the pan and let them sit without touching for about 30 seconds
- Flip the noodles and spread them out again
- repeat 2-3 times until the noodles soak up the sauce, start to caramelize and reach desired tenderness

COMBINE ALL AND STIRFRY

- Add chicken mixture back into pan
- Add the tomatoes and basil and toss to combine
- Stir fry another 1 minute
- Serve immediately
