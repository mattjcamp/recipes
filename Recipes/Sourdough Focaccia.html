Sourdough Focaccia

★★★★★

Signature

Prep Time: 18 hours Cook Time: 30 minutes Total Time: 19 hours Difficulty: Medium Source: @mattjcamp

Ingredients

Levain

1 tbsp sourdough starter

50g lukewarm or warm water

50g unbleached flour

Dough

440g flour

5g salt

75g sourdough levain

50g olive oil

312g water

Directions

Levain

Make the levain the night before by combining the levain ingrediants.

Dough

Combine flour, salt, olive oil, levain, and water in a large bowl. Your dough should be very
wet but don’t worry that’s what we are looking for.

Let it rest for 15 minutes before giving it your first stretch and fold. Continue giving it 2 more stretches and folds every 30 minutes for 1 1/2 hours.

Once your stretch and folding are done, let it rise for about 1 – 2 hours or until it doubles in size.

Prepare Loaf

Using a 9×13 inch baking tray, cover the bottom with parchment paper and drizzle in olive
oil and spread it out in the pan. Make sure to oil up the sides and the corners.

Add in your focaccia dough and spread it out as best you can. Let it rest for about 15 minutes so the gluten structure in the dough can relax and you can stretch it to the full size of your pan.

Once you have stretched your dough out, give it another 15 minutes rest.

Next, drizzle more olive oil over your dough. Dot your dough with cut up sundried tomatoes, olives, and rosemary.

Using a salt brine, which is just 1/4 cup of warm or room temperature water with about 1 teaspoon of salt mixed in until dissolved, start dimpling your bread being sure to push some of your topping into your dough.

Cover your dough and proof for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size. Alternatively, cold-proof in the fridge overnight to develop more flavor.

Bake

Once your dough has risen, preheat your oven to 450 degrees and bake for 25 – 28 minutes. Remove from oven when internal temp reaches 210F.
