Bagels

★★★★

Synced

Prep Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 40 minutes Total Time: 24 hoiurs Difficulty: Hard Servings: 16 to 20 bagels Source: @mattjcamp

Ingredients

DOUGH

690g Bread Flour

454g Lukewarm water

8g Active dry yeast

18g Salt

35g Non-diastatic malt powder

25g Sugar

100g Sourdough Discard, Optional, remove this amount from flour and water

WATER BATH

1814g Water, Half a dutch oven

18g Barley Malt Syrup, 2 tbsp for half a dutch oven 14g Sugar, 1 tbsp for half a dutch oven

1 tbsp Baking Soda

BAKE

Egg wash, beaten egg+water Toppings

Directions

MAKE DOUGH
1. In a large bowl, mix all of the ingredients, cover, and then let the mixture hydrate for 30 minutes.
2. Cut the dough in half and use a food processor for each half separately by pulsing 45 times.
3. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a lightly greased covered bowl. 4. Allow the dough to rise until doubled in bulk 24 to 48 hours in the fridge
COOK
5. Gently deflate the dough, and transfer it to a lightly greased work surface.
6. For the water bath: Put the water into a large, shallow pan; it should be about 3" deep.
Add the malt sryup, baking soda, and molasses (or just sugar). Bring the water to a boil while you're shaping the bagels.
7. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
8. Divide the dough into 8 150 grams for large bagels
9. Working with one piece of dough at a time, shape it into a ball
10. Poke a hole through the center with your index finger, and twirl; the dough will form a ring
11. Place the bagels on a floured parchment-lined baking sheet as they're shaped.
12. Let bagels rest for 20 minutes covered by towel
13. Once all the bagels are shaped, reduce the boiling water bath to a very gentle simmer
14. Starting with the first bagels you shaped, gently transfer 3 or 4 at a time to the water bath; don't crowd them
15. Simmer them for about 30 seconds on each side, then return them to the baking sheet. 16. Put bagels in egg wash and add toppings
17. Bake the bagels for 15 minutes
18. Flip bagels and then cook for another 15 minutes
19. Flip bagels again and cook for another 5 minutes
OPTIONAL
Flip and then five minutes more if they seem to need more crispiness then
turn off the oven but leave the bagels in on a wire rack
21. Bagels are done when they're a deep golden brown and reach a tempature of 210o F.
22. Remove them from the oven, and cool them on a rack and let them sit for a few hours before storing

Notes


- full recipe overpowers motor
- add plenty of flour after shaping to stop sticking
- Use parchment paper not silicon mats to avoid sticking
- Try turning oven off putting bagels on wire rack placing them back for seven minutes to see if they crisp more without burning
