Directions

MAKE DOUGH

In a large bowl, mix all ingredients, cover, and then let the mixture hydrate for 30 minutes

Cut the dough in half and use a food processor for each half separately by pulsing 45 times

Shape the dough into a ball, and place it in a glass-covered bowl

Allow the dough to rise until doubled in bulk 24 to 48 hours in the fridge

WATER BATH

Put the water into a dutch oven, about 5" deep

Add the malt syrup, baking soda, and sugar

Bring the water to a boil while you're shaping the bagels

SHAPE

Preheat the oven to 400°F

Gently deflate the dough, and transfer it to a lightly floured work surface

Divide the dough into 8 150-gram pieces

Working with one piece of dough at a time, shape it into a ball

Roll the dough into a log with thinner tapered ends and then form the log into a circle

Place the bagels on a floured surface as they're shaped

Flour the bagels

Let bagels rest for 20 minutes covered by a towel

COOK

Once all the bagels are shaped, reduce the boiling water bath to a very gentle simmer

Starting with the first bagels you shaped, gently transfer 3 or 4 at a time to the water bath; don't crowd them

Simmer them for about 30 seconds on each side, then return them to the baking sheet

Put bagels in egg wash and add toppings

Bake the bagels for 35 minutes

NOTE Bagels are done when they're a deep golden brown and reach a temperature of 210F

Remove bagels from parchment paper and put them on a wire rake on the baking sheet

Return bagels to oven

Turn off the oven and leave the door open a crack to cure the bagels

Remove them from the oven, cool them on a rack, and let them sit for a few hours before storing

Notes


SOURDOUGH METHOD NOTES
- Hydrate, wait 45 minutes, 2 hours stretch and folds, cold ferment 48 hours. Shape bagels day of.
