Sourdough Country Loaf

★★★★★

Synced

Prep Time: 6 hours Cook Time: 40 minutes Total Time: 36 hours Difficulty: Hard Source: @mattjcamp

Ingredients

Levain

1 tbsp Sourdough Starter

200g Whole Wheat Flour

200g Water

Dough

200g Levain, 20% of flour

700g Water (80F)

900g Bread Flour

100g White Whole Wheat Flour, Alternate: Rye or Regular Whole Wheat

50g Water, Lukewarm at around 80F

20g Kosher Salt

3g Dry Yeast, Alternate: if not using Leven

Directions

Make Levain (Evening Before: 5 minutes)
1. On the night before baking, discard all but one tablespoon of a sourdough starter
2. Feed leven with the flour and water. Levain is ready when a small amount floats in water (it
would have risen 20%)
Hydrate Dough (Next Morning: 45 minutes)
3. Combine the larger portion of water into a large mixing bowl with the Leven and mix to
disperse
4. Add flour and mix to combine and let this mixture rest for ~40 minutes
5. Add salt and smaller water mixture to mixture then use hands to combine mixture 6. Fold the dough on top of itself
Bulk Rise (Morning: 3-4 hours)
7. Try to maintain 78F-82F temperature by storing the dough in the small oven
8. Do turn/fold every 30 minutes for the first two hours (dough will be denser and weak at this point)
9. For the last 2 hours only do gentle turn/folds until by the end of the third hour the dough should be soft and aerated. The dough should release from the bowl and have grown 20- 30% when it is ready
Bench Shaping (Afternoon: 30-60 minutes)
10. Lightly flour outside of dough and then cut dough into sizes for baking
11. Lightly flour remaining exposed dough and then work dough into taunt balls 12. Cover and let dough balls rest for 30 minutes
13. If the dough turns into a flat pancake dripping too much, repeat the shaping step to build more strength
Final Shaping (Afternoon: 10 minutes)
14. Lightly flour dough top surfaces and flip dough so top rests on work surface. Take care to not deflate the dough to preserve oven spring. We are making a series of folds to create tension in the dough
15. Create a neat package by folding the 1/3 dough closest to you over the middle of the round, stretch out dough horizontally to your right and fold this over the center, and stretch out dough horizontally to your left and fold this over the center. Stretch out the third of the dough farthest from you and fold this flap toward you, over the previous folds. Grab the dough nearest to you and wrap it over while rolling the whole package away from you so that the smooth underside of the loaf is now the top and all the seams are on the bottom
16. Cup your hands and pull the dough toward you to tighten the tension
17. Line the shaping bowl with flour and gently place bowl seam side up into proofing
bowls. Pinch the loaf shut if needed to increase tension.
Final Rise (Late Afternoon/Next Morning: 5-48 hours)
18. After 2 hours you would get a mild tasting bread and the longer the dough sits the more
acidic it would be
19. Put in fridge to cold ferment for up to 48 hours (16-18 is ideal) to maximize complexity and slightly more acidic flavor
Bake (Late Afternoon/Next Morning (1 hour)
20. Preheat oven to 500 degrees with dutch oven inside 20 minutes before you are ready to
bake
21. Take dough out of refrigerator and place dough in preheated dutch oven
22. Add flour, score surface of dough, and replace dutch oven cover before placing into the oven
23. Reduce oven temperature to 450F and bake for 20 minutes
24. Remove dutch oven cover and then bake for another 20 minutes (optionally check after 15 minutes if you want a softer crust)
25. Check exterior color and then remove from oven and cool on rack. If the loaf doesn’t appear dark enough leave it in the oven after turning it off with the door open but check on it every five minutes.

Notes

This 70% hydration dough full recipe makes two full classic sourdough loaves. It requires attention throughout the day but not much actual hands on work.
Levain: 20%-25% of flour weight
Dry Active Yeast: 0.3% of flour weight (3 grams yeast per 1000 grams flour)
Oven: middle rack with aluminum sheet between dutch oven and heating element. Remove pizza steal.
Bread Flour, 90%
Whole Wheat Flour, 10% Water, 75%
Levain, 20%
Salt, 2%
Dry Active Yeast, 3%

BAKING ROUTINE
Around 7PM take out the starter and feed it. Optionally use the discard in a recipe. Use the feed starter the next morning to make the dough. Then refeed the starter and return it to the refrigerator after two hours.
